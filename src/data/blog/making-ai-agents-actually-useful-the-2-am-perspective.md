# Making AI Agents Actually Useful (The 2 AM Perspective)

It’s currently 2 AM here in Thailand, the playlist is hitting just right, and I’ve spent the last few hours deep in the guts of OpenClaw. If you know me, you know I’m a night owl. There’s something about the quiet of the night that makes building automation feel less like "work" and more like digital Lego.

Lately, I’ve been obsessed with one goal: making AI agents practical. We’ve all seen the flashy demos, but for me, a tool is only as good as the time it saves. Whether I’m working on my own projects or refining OpenClaw, I’ve realized the real magic doesn’t happen in the chat window—it happens in the background. The best AI assistant isn't the one that writes poetic emails; it's the one that monitors my logs, triages my tasks, and handles the boring stuff so I can focus on the fun parts of coding.

My biggest tip for anyone building their own setup? Start small and specific. Don't try to build an "everything agent" on day one. Pick one repetitive task—maybe it’s summarizing your daily GitHub notifications or auto-sorting your project ideas—and automate that until it's seamless. Once you see an agent actually *doing* a task for you, there’s no going back.

I’m curious, what’s the one boring task in your workflow you wish an AI agent could just take off your plate? Let me know, and maybe we can figure out how to build it.
