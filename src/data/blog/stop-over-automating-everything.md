Been thinking about how much time I waste trying to save time. It's that classic dev trap. You see a task that takes like, thirty seconds. But you have to do it every day. So your brain goes, "Hey, I should write a script for this!" Five hours later, you're debugging some weird edge case in a bash script that's now 200 lines long. And for what? To save thirty seconds? I do this constantly. It's like a sickness. 

I'm exhausted just looking at my GitHub lately. Half my repos are just half-finished "efficiency" tools that I'll never actually use because the manual way was actually fine. I'm trying to get better at just... doing the work. Not everything needs a pipeline. Sometimes you just gotta click the button or copy the text. It's not lazy, it's just being smart with your energy. I'm so tired of fighting my own "solutions." I think I'm gonna delete half my cron jobs today and see if the world ends. It probably won't. 

Do you guys actually use the stuff you automate, or is it just for the vibes?
