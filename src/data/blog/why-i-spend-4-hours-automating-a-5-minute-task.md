You know what sucks? Spending four hours writing a script to save yourself five minutes of work. I did it again last night. I was supposed to be sleeping, but I found this tiny, annoying task that I do every morning. It’s just clicking a few buttons and moving some files. Instead of just doing it, I stayed up until 2 AM trying to make it happen automatically. 

I’m exhausted, but honestly, it’s kind of a rush when it finally works. It’s that feeling of "I never have to think about this again." Even if I spent way more time building it than I’ll ever save. It’s like a weird game I play with myself. My friends think I’m crazy, but they don't get the satisfaction of a clean terminal and a job well done by a bot I built. 

I think I’m addicted to the process, not even the result. Now I’m just staring at my screen with coffee #3, wondering what else I can break by trying to "fix" it. Does anyone else get stuck in this loop where you'd rather write a hundred lines of code than do one manual thing?
